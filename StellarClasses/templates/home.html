What Can We Classify?

Three types of cosmic objects our model recognizes

⭐
STAR
Stars

Stars are massive balls of hot plasma held together by gravity and powered by nuclear fusion at their cores. They emit light across all wavelengths. Our own Sun is a star. Stars have very low redshift values since they are inside our galaxy.

Fun Fact
The largest known star, UY Scuti, is so big that if placed at the center of our Solar System, it would extend past Jupiter's orbit.
🌌
GALAXY
Galaxies

Galaxies are enormous systems containing billions of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter. They come in spiral, elliptical, and irregular shapes. The Milky Way is our home galaxy. Galaxies have moderate redshift values.

Fun Fact
The observable universe contains an estimated 2 trillion galaxies — that's more galaxies than there are grains of sand on all of Earth's beaches.
💫
QUASAR
Quasars

Quasars (QSOs) are the brightest objects in the universe — powered by supermassive black holes consuming surrounding matter. They sit at extreme distances from Earth and are identified by their extremely high redshift values.

Fun Fact
The most luminous quasar ever discovered, J0529-4351, shines 500 trillion times brighter than our Sun.
