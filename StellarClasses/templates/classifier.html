What do these values mean?

Input Guide
Alpha & Delta

Sky coordinates — like longitude and latitude but for space. Alpha is right ascension (0–360°), Delta is declination (−90° to +90°).

Alpha: 0 to 360 · Delta: −90 to +90
U, G, R, I, Z — Filter Bands

These are how bright the object appears through 5 different light filters — from ultraviolet (u) to infrared (z). Lower number = brighter object.

Typical range: 10 (very bright) to 30 (very faint)
Redshift — The Most Important Value

How fast the object is moving away from us. Stars have near-zero redshift. Galaxies have moderate values. Quasars have extremely high values.

⭐ Star: ~0.0001  ·  🌌 Galaxy: 0.05–1.0  ·  💫 Quasar: 1.0–7.0
